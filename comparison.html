The Random Forest baseline achieved a significantly lower false positive rate (0.09%) compared to the DNN-BiLSTM-Attention model (3.08%). This result is consistent with Ali et al. (2025), who found that ensemble tree-based methods outperform deep learning on structured tabular network flow data. At a typical SOC volume of 10,000 alerts per day, this difference translates to approximately 299 fewer false alerts per day and an estimated 25 hours of analyst time saved per day.
